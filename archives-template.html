Over the years, the “Year Of” program has provided a framework for looking at the relationship between science and religion, humans and our environments, the relationships between self and society, methods of conflict transformation, and – this year – the connections between self and society.

During this Year of Self and Society, we have been hosting events, discussions, activities, and academic explorations of the ways self and society interact, conflict, cross boundaries, create lines of division, interplay, and thus affect one another and our world.

The title provides the frame for topics that encourage academic debate as the college aims to consider:

  • The definitions of “self” and “society”
  • Explore issues related to self and society
  • Critically examine society’s impact on human thought, health, and behavior
