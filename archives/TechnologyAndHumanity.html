TECHNOLOGY & HUMANITY

2013-2014

Over the years, the “Year Of” program has provided a framework for looking at the relationship between science and religion, humans and our environments, the relationships between self and society, methods of conflict transformation, and – this year – the connections between humanity and technology.

During this Year of Humanity and Technology, we have been hosting events, discussions, activities, and academic explorations of the ways technology and humanity interact, conflict, cross boundaries, create lines of division, interplay, and thus affect one another and our world.

The title provides the frame for topics that encourage academic debate as the college aims to consider:

  • the definitions of “humanity” and “technology”
  • explore issues related to technology
  • investigate technology's impact on human thought, health, and behavior
  • critically examine the history and future of technology and its relationship to individuals, communities, governments, and the world.
