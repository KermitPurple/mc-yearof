The Universe & How We Know It

28

JAN 2015

Thursday, January 29, 7-8:30pm
EATM Auditorium

The collective efforts of the soft tissue that lies within our skulls has engendered an enormous amount of speculation about what is going on outside of these skulls. Given the many accounts that humans have developed to explain the universe, how can we distinguish the tall tales that many dismiss as “myth” from those insights that may point us to “reality”? Is it even possible to make this distinction, or are we doomed to blindly make our way through a universe that is ultimately beyond our understanding?

Because it regards humans as biological organisms whose primary way of adapting to the world is through culture, anthropology offers a way out of this impasse. In this presentation, Professor John Baker will consider some of the ways that a brain may acquire its knowledge about the world “out there” and the way that this brain may share its knowledge with others. Knowledge, we will see, is both intensely personal and collectively created. How this knowledge is then corroborated is the key to understanding both “myth” and “reality.” For more information, contact Svetlana Kasalovic at skasalovic@vcccd.edu.
