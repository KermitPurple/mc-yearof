From Benjamin Franklin to Kim Kardashian

12

NOV 2014

Wednesday, November 12, 2014
COM 129 (TV STUDIO) 12:00pm-12:50pm

From Benjamin Franklin and Horatio Alger to MTV Cribs and Kim Kardashian, American popular culture has had a long tradition of celebrating icons of “success.” As the Internet and Youtube have become more and more integrated into our everyday lives, there is a new trend of icon emerging. How has American culture’s depiction of success changed from the “self-made men” of Franklin’s era to today’s Internet celebrities whose success depends of generating billions of “clicks”? What does this new trend tell us about the changing nature of success in America?

Join Professors Jeremy Kaye and Danielle Kaprelian in connecting some of Malcolm Gladwell’s provocative ideas about successful people in Outliers to current trends within American popular culture. For more information, contact Svetlana Kasalovic at skasalovic@vcccd.edu.
