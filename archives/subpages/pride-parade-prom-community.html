PRIDE: Parade, Prom, Community

25

FEB 2016

February 25, 2016
1:00pm – 2:30pm at PS 202

Panel Discussion with Saul Bromberger, Sandra Hoover and MC Spectrum Club

In the 1980s, the once unorganized LGBTQ+ community organized to fight against homophobia, transphobia, the AIDS epidemic, and greater representation. One of the greatest ways that those activist leaders fought those battles was through the very act of self-love, and through the act of PRIDE! Artists Saul Bromberger and Sandra Hoover captured this PRIDE in their series of photographs “San Francisco Gay and Lesbian Freedom Day Parade: 1984-1990.” This photo essay is currently on exhibition at the Moorpark College Art Gallery along with the series “The Gay Prom: Portraits of LBGTQ Teens in Our New World” 2014-2015. In the panel discussion, photographers Saul and Sandra will join members of the MC Spectrum club to discuss pride in the LBGTQ community: then and now and what the MC community can learn from these representations of self-love.

For more information, contact Katrina McElroy at kmcelroy@vcccd.edu.
