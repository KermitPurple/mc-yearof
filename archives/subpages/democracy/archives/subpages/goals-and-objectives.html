SEP 2010

Goals and Objectives

Students, faculty and staff will explain their cultural, philosophical, political, religious and spiritual commitments, when appropriate, for the sake of openness and dialogue.

Students, faculty and staff will actively and empathetically seek to understand the cultural, philosophical, political, religious and spiritual commitments of those with whom they regularly interact so that their differences might be mutually enriching.

Implementation

Students, faculty and staff will analyze past and present conflicts, both political and personal, in order to extract principles that can apply to their own situations.

Venues

Students, faculty and staff will consider the abovein all aspects of campus life, e.g., class activities, learning communities, college committees and day-to-day interactions.

Evaluation

A brief questionnaire will follow any activity where the above is tried.
