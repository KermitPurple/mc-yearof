Free Speech on Campus: Myth or Reality?

3

JUN 2015

April 23, 2015
1–2:30pm at EATM Auditorium

For centuries, college campuses have been considered bastions of critical discourse, spaces where the open exchange of ideas was not simply encouraged but mandated. In recent decades, however, the notion of “political correctness” on college campuses has become a lightning rod for debate and at times a way to censor those with opposing viewpoints.

Join Professors Nils Slattum and Sona Dombourian in separating myth from reality when it comes to free speech on campus. What constitutes “free speech” on college campuses, and what types of speech are outlawed? How do professors manage differing perspective in the classroom in order to create a thriving critical discourse? How do students feel when their perspectives differ from those of their professors’ and other classmates’? How are free speech debates on college campuses becoming a microcosm for debate within the larger society? For more information, contact Jeremy Kaye at jkaye@vcccd.edu.
