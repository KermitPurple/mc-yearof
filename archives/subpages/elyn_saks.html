Elyn Saks: Living with Schizophrenia

17

NOV 2014

Thursday, November 20, 1-2pm
EATM Auditorium

She first began to have symptoms at age 16 and five years later, had her first full psychotic break while in law school. To this day, without appropriate treatment, she experiences paranoid delusions which take over her world. Elyn Saks is also Professor of Law, Psychology, and Psychiatry and the Behavioral Sciences at USC. “Everything about this illness says I shouldn’t be here. But I am.” Come hear Elyn Saks talk about defying the myths of mental illness.

In the Year of Myth and Reality, this event opens up the myths and the realities about living with schizophrenia. Professor Saks will join us to respond to questions. For more information, contact Svetlana Kasalovic at skasalovic@vcccd.edu
