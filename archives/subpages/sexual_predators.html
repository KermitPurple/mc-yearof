Sexual Predators and Women: Myths and Realities

6

NOV 2014

Thursday, November 6, 1-2pm
EATM Auditorium

Will a sexual predator target you? Who are they? What are the probabilities that you will be attacked? What can women do to reduce the risk of being attacked?

Join us in a frank and informative discussion of the myths and realities of sexual predators and their victimization of women. Visualize the common tactics employed by sexual predators. Explore risky behaviors and situations you should avoid. Understand how to uncover and trust your natural ability to sense and avoid dangerous persons, before they attack. Empower yourself to summon the strength and determination to survive a violent encounter.

For the past 20 years, Chad Basile has served as a Special Agent of the Los Angles Division of the FBI. He is an FBI Master Police Instructor and has trained and certified more than two thousand law enforcement and military firearms and defensive tactics instructors. He is a personal student of Grandmaster, Dr. Masaaki Hatsumi, headmaster and creator of the Japanese Bujinkan System. Chad has trained and and studied martial arts for more than 35 years and holds master level ranks in a number of martial art styles. Chad is also certified as a Master Firearms and Defensive Tactics Police Instructor of the European Union, where he has taught and certified European Law Enforcement and Military instructors. Locally, he is an Adjunct Professor of Criminal Justice at Moorpark College. Chad regularly holds self protection seminars for victims of domestic violence and health care professionals. Prior to his FBI appointment, Chad was a civil litigation attorney practicing law in Northern California.
