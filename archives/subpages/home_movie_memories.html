Wednesday, December 3 10am-12:30pm
FORUM

Every family has its stories; every family has its secrets. What happens when those stories and secrets collide, forcing a family to reconstruct and reimagine their history? How does one recreate their memories and perceptions of the past when family myths have been exposed as lies? What kind of reality is left in the wake of such knowledge? Sarah Polley’s personal film, Stories We Tell (2012), seeks answers to these difficult questions, visualizing the depth of memory, love and loss through the “performative mode” of documentary. Join Film Studies professor, Alison Hoffman-Han, for a screening and discussion of Polley’s award- winning, boundary-blurring documentary.
