Success in the Visual Arts: Myth or Reality?

21

APR 2015

Thursday, April 9 1:00-2:30 pm
EATM Auditorium

Moderators: Erika Lizée and Cynthia Minet (Art Faculty at Moorpark College)

What does it mean to be successful in the visual arts? How do we define success? There are infinite paths in the visual arts, whether teaching, working commercially, engaging with the gallery system, writing about art, working in the entertainment industry, etc. How artists support themselves financially is just one of the markers of success. The Year of Myth and Reality hosts a panel with four artists who work in varied fields of the visual arts. Come take part in the discussion following the presentations of these creative individuals:

Kim Abeles’ installations and community projects cross disciplines and media to explore biography, geography and environment. Abeles has exhibited her large scale installations in 20+ countries, holds an MFA from UC Irvine, and she is also Professor Emeritus from CSUN. Among her many honors, Abeles has been recipient of the Guggenheim Memorial Fellowship, J. Paul Getty Trust Fund for the Visual Arts, California Community Foundation, Pollack-Krasner Foundation, and the California Arts Council.

Martin Cox is a Los Angeles-based photographer who works both in the fine arts and in commercial photography. A graduate of the Exeter College of Art and Design in England, Cox’s photographs have been exhibited in galleries in Los Angeles, London and in festivals, most recently in PhotoLA. He just completed a commission for the Los Angeles Maritime Museum, and is preparing for a solo show at the LA City Council offices in Echo Park.

Tulsa Kinney is the editor and publisher of Artillery, a contemporary art magazine based in Los Angeles. She received her MFA at USC in 1988 and showed her paintings and videos in numerous solo and group exhibitions for over two decades. Tulsa started writing about 15 years ago and her articles have been published in the Los Angeles Times and LA Weekly. She continues to write about art and stay deeply immersed in the contemporary art world.

Michael Cedeno is known for his character design, animation, and supervision at Disney Feature Animation, where he worked for 27 years. He animated principle characters on The Little Mermaid, Aladdin, Pocahontas, Lion King, Beauty and the Beast, Atlantis and others. Cedeno also worked at other studios in LA (Warner Bros, Universal.) He studied at Cal Arts (Animation) and is currently is a freelance designer, animator and a fine artist painting in the plein air tradition. He has taught painting, character design, and life drawing at Otis.
