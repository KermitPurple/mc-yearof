Let’s Get Physical (and Mental)

29

OCT 2015

With Allison Case Barton, Health Educator, Chester Lin, the Tai Chi teacher, and Sydney Sims, Chair of the English Department

Originating in Asia, mindfulness, yoga, and tai chi are practices that have endured through the ages for their healthful and curative qualities. These practices connect breath with mind, body, and emotions to promote physi- cal and mental health, well-being, and focus.

If you can breathe and move your body you have all the qualifications you need to begin. Come join us for one, two, or all three sessions in this series to discover a new path to wellness.

For more information, contact Allison Case Barton at abarton@vcccd.edu.

Mindfulness Training

November 5, 2015
1:00pm – 2:30pm at FH 218

Mindfulness is a method of training your brain to increase your capacity to be calm, alert and focused, and to be more aware of your present-moment experience. It has been shown to improve students’ memory and performance in class and on tests.

Come join us for an introductory session. Bring your open mind and willingness to try something new.

Tai Chi

November 12, 2015
1:00pm – 2:30pm at 1-G 122

Taichi is a traditional Chinese practice known for its slow, gentle, and graceful movements. Originally a method of self defense, many people today practice it as a moving meditation to improve their balance, ease of movement, and stress.

Come join us for an introductory session. Tai Chi is best practiced in comfortable clothing, and in bare feet or socks.

Yoga

November 19, 2015
12:00pm – 12:50pm at PA 107

Yoga is part of an integrated system of physical and spiritual wellness, healing, and growth. In hatha yoga we move through a sequence of postures and stillness, coordinated with the breath, that encourage ease and self-awareness. Join us for a mild session to promote flexibility, range of motion, calmness, and harmony.

Bring a mat or towel, wear loose or stretchy clothes, and eat lightly beforehand.
