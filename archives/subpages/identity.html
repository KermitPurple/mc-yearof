Identity, Love and Romance in Virtual Worlds

15

OCT 2014

Wednesday, October 15 12:00-12:50pm
COM 129 (TV STUDIO)

Virtual worlds and Massive Multiplayer Online (MMO) games such as Second Life and World of Warcraft are places where the boundaries between what is “real” and what is “virtual” can be blurred immensely. People create avatars of themselves, or potentially a whole other person, enter these worlds and forge relationships with others. “Reality” blurs as people make connections, find love and share lives in a world populated solely on a virtual level. As part of the Year of Myth and Reality, Sociology professor Dan Vieira will look at issues of identity, love and relationship in such virtual worlds, stressing that the line between what is “real” and what is “mythic” can be hazy indeed in such online places. For more information, contact Svetlana Kasalovic at skasalovic@vcccd.edu.
