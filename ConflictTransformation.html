Conflict Transformation

2011-2012

Over the years, the “Year Of” program has provided a framework for looking at the relationship between science and religion, humans and our environments, the relationships between self and society, methods of conflict transformation, and – this year – conflict transformation.

Rather than being threatening, conflicts can be understood as providing opportunities to increase understanding between individuals, between groups, and between cultures. This Year of Conflict transformation will investigate the potential for constructive change inherent in conflict. Conflict transformation encourages the building of multiple frameworks, so that individuals can engage in deeper reflection about the origin and structure of our conflicts. Conflicts can be opportunities for growth.

The co-chairs for this year are Janice Daurio (Philosophy) and Jack Miller (Political Science/Urban Studies).
